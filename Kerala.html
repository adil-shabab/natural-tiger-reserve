Periyar Tiger Reserve

Periyar Tiger Reserve is located in the southern Indian state of Kerala, in the Western Ghats mountain range. Here's more information about it:

Location: Periyar Tiger Reserve is situated in the districts of Idukki and Pathanamthitta in Kerala. It covers an area of approximately 925 square kilometers.

Establishment: The reserve was established in 1982 and was declared a Tiger Reserve in 1978 under India's Project Tiger initiative. It is one of the oldest tiger reserves in India.

Biodiversity: Periyar Tiger Reserve is known for its rich biodiversity. Apart from Bengal tigers, it is home to other wildlife species such as Indian elephants, Indian leopards, sambar deer, Indian bison (gaur), wild boars, and a variety of bird species.

Habitat: The landscape of Periyar Tiger Reserve comprises tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests, grasslands, and water bodies. The reserve is centered around the Periyar Lake, which provides vital water sources and supports a variety of aquatic life.

Conservation Efforts: Periyar Tiger Reserve is actively involved in conservation initiatives aimed at protecting its biodiversity and ensuring the survival of its flagship species, including the Bengal tiger. Conservation efforts include habitat preservation, anti-poaching measures, community involvement, and research and monitoring programs.

Tourism: The reserve offers various eco-tourism activities for visitors to explore its natural beauty and wildlife. Activities include boat safaris on Periyar Lake, guided nature walks, bamboo rafting, and trekking. Periyar is also a popular destination for birdwatching and nature photography.

Accessibility: Periyar Tiger Reserve is accessible from major cities in Kerala such as Kochi and Thiruvananthapuram. The nearest airport is the Kochi International Airport, while the nearest railway station is in Kottayam.

Overall, Periyar Tiger Reserve is an important conservation area in India, known for its biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and conservation efforts aimed at protecting its natural heritage, including the majestic Bengal tiger.

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve

Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is located in the Palakkad district of Kerala, India, and adjoining regions of Tamil Nadu. Here's more information about it:

Location: Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is situated in the Western Ghats mountain range, spanning across the Palakkad district of Kerala and the Coimbatore district of Tamil Nadu. It covers an area of approximately 643.66 square kilometers.

Establishment: The reserve was established in 1973 and was declared a Tiger Reserve in 2010 under India's Project Tiger initiative.

Biodiversity: Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is known for its rich biodiversity. Apart from Bengal tigers, it is home to other wildlife species such as Indian elephants, Indian leopards, sloth bears, Indian gaurs (bison), sambar deer, spotted deer, and a variety of bird species.

Habitat: The landscape of Parambikulam Tiger Reserve comprises tropical and subtropical moist broadleaf forests, grasslands, and water bodies. The reserve is characterized by hilly terrain, valleys, and several dams and reservoirs, including the Parambikulam Dam and the Thunakadavu Dam.

Conservation Efforts: Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is actively involved in conservation initiatives aimed at protecting its biodiversity and ensuring the survival of its flagship species, including the Bengal tiger. Conservation efforts include habitat preservation, anti-poaching measures, community involvement, and research and monitoring programs.

Tourism: The reserve offers various eco-tourism activities for visitors to explore its natural beauty and wildlife. Activities include guided safaris, boat rides, trekking, bamboo rafting, and nature walks. Parambikulam is also a popular destination for birdwatching and nature photography.

Accessibility: Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is accessible from major cities in Kerala and Tamil Nadu such as Coimbatore and Palakkad. The nearest airport is the Coimbatore International Airport, while the nearest railway station is in Palakkad.

Overall, Parambikulam Tiger Reserve is an important conservation area in South India, known for its biodiversity, scenic landscapes, and conservation efforts aimed at protecting its natural heritage, including the majestic Bengal tiger.
